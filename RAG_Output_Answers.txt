Answer: According to the State Travel Expense Act (LRKG), an official trip is a journey undertaken to conduct official business outside the usual place of work, which has been ordered or approved by the responsible superior, unless an order or approval is not applicable due to the nature of the official’s office or the nature of the official business. Finally, trips between the domestic territory and foreign countries, or within foreign countries with at least one business location abroad, are considered official trips abroad.
